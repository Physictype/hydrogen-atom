Schrödinger's equation is an equation in Quantum Physics that describes how particles evolve based on their potential energy function. It is written like so: $$i\hbar\frac{\partial\psi}{\partial t}=-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2\psi+V\psi.$$ Here, \(\psi\) is the wave function, which describes where the particle is, based on probability. It looks very scary, but don't be scared—all this is describing, in more complicated terms, is that total energy is kinetic plus potential.
